Nintendo

Nintendo has been a huge name in the home video game industry since the 1980s. Ever since then, they have released multiple home consoles, handheld consoles, and other different video-game-related accessories. Their most recent console is known as the Nintendo Switch. Currently, the Nintendo Switch has multiple different versions that operate in different capacities, but they all play the same games.

Features

The biggest selling point for the Nintendo Switch is its identity as a hybrid console. It can be played on any modern television with an HDMI and it is able to disconnect from its dock to be played on the go. The base model Nintendo Switch and the Switch OLED has a portable battery life of approximately 4 to 8 hours depending on the game, while the Switch Lite only has about 3 to 7 hours of battery life.

The controllers on the Nintendo Switch and Switch OLED are modular controllers with multiple different configurations depending on how you play the game. It can be put together as one controller, used when attached to the main screen of the console, or even split apart and used as 2 separate controllers that can be shared with someone else.

Many of the games offered on the Nintendo Switch family are family friendly and make great choices to give to kids of all ages. They have many long-lasting franchises that people have heard of over the years such as Super Mario and Pokémon. They remain as the company with some of the longest lasting and highest grossing game titles of all time.

The Nintendo Switch also has an online membership program that has its own benefits, such as the ability to play Nintendo games online with people and a catalogue of older games from the Nintendo Entertainment System and Super Nintendo. It is also one of the cheaper membership programs available.

Drawbacks

Overall, the Nintendo Switch doesn't have the same kind of graphical output that most game consoles possess. The performance of the Switch is a bit lacking, which sometimes results in games slowing down or stuttering.

While Nintendo has an online membership program, some of the features that it uses aren't well integrated, such as needing friend codes to play with friends and voice chat being regulated as a separate application on your smartphone.

One of the biggest problems with Nintendo Switch controllers is a phenomenon known as “drift.” Over time as the analog sticks on the controller are used, it may begin to degrade to a point where it detects movement when none is present. You may need to replace the controllers after a year or so.

Games to Play

Super Smash Bros. Ultimate: This is a fighting game that stars many characters from across multiple video game franchises. It has multiple different game modes, multiplayer, and over 80 characters to play.

Super Mario Odyssey: This is a platformer starring Mario, one of the most famous video game characters of all time. It is a fun adventure across multiple different worlds and great controls.

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild: This is an open world adventure game from the Legend of Zelda franchise. You explore the land of Hyrule defeating monsters and grow stronger in order to save the world from the evil Calamity Ganon.
