Sony PlayStation 5

As a major company that produces many kinds of electronic devices, Sony has become one of the biggest names in the tech industry. They first introduced the original PlayStation just in time for the holiday season back in 1994. Ever since then, they have been a strong force in the videogames industry as well, producing many different home and handheld consoles. After 4 different home consoles, Sony released their newest console towards the end of 2020, known simply as the PlayStation 5.

Features

The PlayStation 5 comes in 2 different models: One with a disk drive (pictured here) and one without. While the one without the disk drive is $100 cheaper than the one with the disk drive, the one with the disk drive allows you to acquire games with the potential to resell or refund games on a disc that you may not enjoy or want to return. The disk drive is also compatible as a disc player for Blu-Ray discs, dvds, and other supported media discs.

The technology behind the PlayStation 5 also has a lot of power behind it. It can keep cool running more advanced games while hardly making a sound. It has insanely fast loading times on many games, so you don't have to worry about waiting too long to enjoy whatever game you're playing.

PlayStation also offers an additional membership program that has different features depending on which program you select. These can range from free monthly games to exclusive deals on other games.

The controller as well is an interesting feature for the console. One of the features of the controller is called “Haptic Feedback.” Basically, it gives the shoulder buttons on the back of the controller resistance when you pull the triggers to give you a more immersive feel to whatever PS5 game you are playing.

Drawbacks

The PlayStation 5 itself is a rather large console. While it is designed not to overheat, it goes about this (partially) by taking up more space and allowing it to ventilate better.

While you can get free games with the monthly subscription programs they offer, it is from a list of pre-selected games. While you may end up with amazing game choices from time to time, you may also be disappointed by the selection.

Unfortunately, the earlier model controllers for the PlayStation 5 also occaisionally suffer from what is known as "stick drift." This means that the controller can register movement on the thumb-sticks due to damage, dust, or crumbs getting into the controller. However, regular cleaning and maintenance can help alleviate the issue.

Games to Play

Astro's Playroom: This is a free game that is included on every PS5. It is a charming platformer game that shows off the major features of the PS5 while giving you a bit of history with PlayStation itself.

Demon's Souls: This is a remake of an older game from the PlayStation 3. It's a roleplaying game with excellent visuals and difficult, but engaging combat. If you are relatively new to video games, I would recommend caution when purchasing it because the game has a reputation for its difficulty.

Ratchet and Clank: Rift Apart: This is a continuation of the Ratchet and Clank series that have been around since the PlayStation 2 era. It is a cartoonish style action/adventure/platforming game with lots of interesting weapons and worlds to explore.
